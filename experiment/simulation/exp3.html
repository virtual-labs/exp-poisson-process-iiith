Properties of Inter-arrival Times

Instructions

This module explores two fundamental properties of a Poisson process: the distribution of its inter-arrival times and their statistical independence.

Objective

  • To observe that the time \(T\) between consecutive events in a Poisson process with rate \(\lambda\) follows an Exponential distribution.
  • To visually verify that these inter-arrival times, \(T_i\) and \(T_{i+1}\), are independent of each other.

Controls

  • Poisson Rate (\(\lambda\)): Adjust the average number of arrivals per unit of time using the slider.
  • Start / Stop / Reset: Control the flow of the simulation.

Visualization

  • Arrivals Timeline: Shows the real-time occurrence of events.
  • Distribution Analysis: Compares the histogram of observed inter-arrival times (in blue) with the theoretical Exponential PDF, \(f(t) = \lambda e^{-\lambda t}\) (in orange).
  • Independence Analysis: A scatter plot of consecutive inter-arrival times \((T_i, T_{i+1})\). Independence is suggested if the points form a random cloud with no clear pattern.

Interpretation

  • As more events are recorded, the empirical histogram should more closely match the shape of the theoretical exponential curve.
  • The best-fit line in the scatter plot should have a slope close to zero, confirming that the length of one waiting period does not predict the length of the next.
