Merging and Splitting of a Poisson Process

Instructions
  • Two independent Poisson emitters operate at rates \(\lambda_1\) and \(\lambda_2\).
  • Merge: Combines both streams into one with rate \(\lambda_1 + \lambda_2\). Each second, a Poisson random sample with this parameter is drawn.
  • Adjust \(\lambda_1\) and \(\lambda_2\) using the sliders. Click “Merge” to see the effect when two poisson processes are merged.
  • Left Panel: Visualizes emitters merging. Each emission releases a particle icon that scatters randomly.
  • Right Panel:: Two real-time charts:
    1. Live line chart: emissions per second.
    2. Histogram: empirical distribution of emission counts.
  • Observations Panel:
    • Running average emission rate vs theoretical \(\lambda\).
    • Real-time absolute error \(| \text{empirical} - \text{theoretical} |\).
